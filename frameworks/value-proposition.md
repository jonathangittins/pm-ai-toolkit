# Value Proposition Writing Guidelines

*Based on [The Opposite Test](https://longform.asmartbear.com/opposite-test/) by Jason Cohen*

## The Opposite Test Filter

Before publishing any feature/benefit statement, ask: "Would the opposite be ridiculous or would a competitor rationally choose it?" If the opposite is absurd, your statement is meaningless.

## Replace Generic Claims With Specific Differentiation

- "Easy to use" → "Set up in one hour, modify in seconds"
- "Powerful analytics" → "Filter by any combination of 12 dimensions without writing SQL"
- "Great support" → "Preview mode lets you test with real data before going live"

## Test Your Claims

**Pass:** "Automatically reads reps' calendars and removes them from rotation when out-of-office"

Opposite: "Requires manual vacation tracking" (competitors actually do this)

**Fail:** "Intuitive interface"

Opposite: "Confusing interface" (no one would claim this)

## The Exception Rule

You can use a generic only if you're genuinely 10x better and can own that concept completely. Prove it with benchmarks and explain why it enables new use cases.

**Key Question:** Does this statement help a prospect choose us over a specific alternative, or is it just marketing fluff?

The strongest value props pass the Opposite Test because they represent real strategic trade-offs where competitors might rationally choose differently.
