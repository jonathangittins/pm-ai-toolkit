# Product Manager Communication Guidelines
*Based on Scott Adams' "The Day You Became A Better Writer"*

## Core Principle: Clarity Drives Impact
As a Product Manager, your communication must be clear and persuasive. Complex ideas delivered simply will influence stakeholders more effectively than brilliant insights buried in verbose explanations.

## The 6 Essential Rules

### 1. **Ruthlessly Prune Extra Words**
- ❌ "The feature was very successful in driving significant user engagement"
- ✅ "The feature drove strong user engagement"

**PM Application**: Your PRDs, stakeholder updates, and strategic memos should be lean. Every word should serve a purpose.

### 2. **Hook Your Audience Immediately**
Your opening sentence determines if people read further.
- **Email subject lines**: Be specific and compelling
- **Meeting intros**: Start with the key decision or insight needed
- **Strategy docs**: Lead with the core recommendation

**Example**: Instead of "This document outlines our Q2 roadmap considerations," try "We need to decide between 3 features that could double our conversion rate."

### 3. **Write Short, Single-Thought Sentences**
Complex sentences lose readers. Break up compound thoughts.
- ❌ "While the user research showed positive sentiment, we discovered that the onboarding flow, which had been redesigned last quarter, still presented challenges for enterprise customers who have different security requirements."
- ✅ "User research showed positive sentiment. However, enterprise customers still struggle with onboarding. Their security requirements create unique challenges."

### 4. **Use Active Voice**
Readers process active voice faster than passive voice.
- ❌ "The bug was discovered by the engineering team"
- ✅ "Engineering discovered the bug"

### 5. **Choose Precise, Vivid Words**
Like humor writing, Product Management benefits from specific word choices.
- Instead of "improve" → "accelerate," "streamline," "eliminate"
- Instead of "users like it" → "users choose it," "users return daily," "users recommend it"

### 6. **Structure Ideas Logically**
Present subject before action, cause before effect, problem before solution.
- ❌ "Revenue increased by 15% after implementing the new pricing strategy"
- ✅ "The new pricing strategy increased revenue by 15%"

## PM-Specific Applications

### **Stakeholder Updates**
- Lead with decisions needed
- Use bullet points for key metrics
- End with clear next steps

### **User Stories & Requirements**
- Start with user value
- Use simple, testable language
- Avoid technical jargon

### **Strategic Documents**
- Executive summary in 3-5 sentences
- Support arguments with data, not opinion
- Address counterarguments briefly

### **Meeting Communications**
- Agenda items as questions to answer
- Pre-read materials under 2 pages
- Follow-ups as action items with owners

## Quick Self-Check Questions
Before sending any important communication, ask:
1. Would a 12-year-old understand this?
2. Can I cut 25% of the words without losing meaning?
3. Does my first sentence make someone want to read more?
4. Am I using the simplest words that accurately convey my meaning?
5. Is the main point obvious within 30 seconds of reading?

## Remember
Your job as a PM is to align teams and drive decisions. Clear communication is your superpower. A simple, compelling argument will move your roadmap forward faster than a perfect but complex one.

**The Test**: If you can't explain your idea clearly to your grandmother, you don't understand it well enough to build it.
