# LNO Effectiveness Framework

Source: [Shreyas Doshi](https://x.com/shreyas/status/1399061782560350208) | [Detailed writeup](https://www.aakashg.com/lno-framework-for-product-managers/)

All tasks are not created equal. Classify every task as one of three types and allocate effort accordingly.

## Leverage (L)

Work that multiplies your effectiveness 10x. Foundational, strategic, high-impact.

- Vision and strategy
- Product roadmapping
- Voice of customer / discovery
- Key design decisions

**Approach**: Strive for excellence. This is where perfectionism pays off.

## Neutral (N)

Important work delivering incremental results. The movement is in inches, not miles.

- Explaining vision to stakeholders
- Polishing decks (content over form)
- Unblocking engineers on execution details
- Medium-priority leadership requests

**Approach**: C+/B- standard. Good work, not excellent. Don't let perfect be the enemy of good.

## Overhead (O)

Necessary but low-impact administrative work. Business expenses of being a PM.

- Project management and coordination
- Note-taking and status tracking
- Reformatting specs into different templates
- Converting emails to tickets

**Approach**: Do a mediocre job – your baseline competence suffices. Better yet, eliminate entirely through team empowerment or automation.

## Application

1. Before starting a task, classify it as L, N, or O
2. Redistribute time: reduce overhead minutes, reallocate to leverage
3. Social pressure to do overhead well intensifies in larger orgs – resist
4. In delivery-focused orgs, "leverage" may need redefining for context

## LNOD Variation

Add **D** for Distractions/Don'ts – tasks to actively decline and redirect.
